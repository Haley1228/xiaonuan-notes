## related work精炼

Multimodal Sentiment Analysis (MSA) has evolved through several paradigms to integrate text, audio, and visual signals, including high-order fusion~\cite{48,21}, correlation-based alignment~\cite{33}, and cross-modal Transformer-based methods~\cite{12,50}. To address modality inconsistency, prior works further explore disentangled representation learning~\cite{13,44,28} and modality rebalancing strategies~\cite{14}, while recent approaches such as DEVA~\cite{38} convert non-verbal cues into textual descriptions for fine-grained enhancement. Meanwhile, multimodal fusion research has shifted from global correlation modeling to interaction-aware frameworks inspired by Partial Information Decomposition (PID)~\cite{3}, where methods like I2MoE~\cite{41} explicitly decompose multimodal information into uniqueness, redundancy, and synergy via expert specialization and tailored interaction losses~\cite{16,31}. However, these methods typically operate at the modality level and overlook fine-grained structural dependencies among multimodal cues.

In parallel, Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)~\cite{37,40} have been adopted to model complex cross-modal relationships, but stacked GAT-based architectures~\cite{37} suffer from over-smoothing~\cite{39}, leading to reduced feature heterogeneity and degraded fine-grained representations. Recent efforts~\cite{25,30} mitigate this issue via single-layer multi-hop diffusion. Building upon these directions, we formulate fine-grained multimodal interaction as a structured cue modeling problem and propose a psychology-guided graph-based framework with pattern-reinforced diffusion and interaction-aware disentanglement, enabling more precise modeling of modality-unique, complementary, redundant, and synergistic information. See Appendix~A for detailed formulations and analysis.
